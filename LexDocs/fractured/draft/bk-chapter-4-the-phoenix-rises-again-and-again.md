Hugh Palmer was the grandfather that I never had who happened to live down the road just like a grandfather would. Hugh Palmer was the owner of Palmer and son excavating in which is only son little Huey work for him full-time. who lived in a trailer a mile and a half away and it was on the low ground, although it was never really it at risk of flooding.

Hugh had a sense of humor and was also the source of many jokes. I even got in trouble for writing to a penpal, a dirty limerick that Hugh taught me.

I can still hear Hugh describing the temperaments of myself and my siblings, that Sean could go from 0 to 100 in a second but he would calm right back down. I've learned to control my emotions more than when I was nine but a lot still holds true with that perspective. He had grandchildren, but in a lot of ways, the cutter kids were closer to Hugh than his grandchildren.

On the academic side of things, I never had to be told to do good in school or get done. I've always had that spark that drives me to do my best and do better than others. The competitive fire in me burns brighter than ever, but that fire started when I was just a kid. I had a sense for where I was at in the intelligence scale and it wasn't at the very top, but it was towards the top. And if I was going to be good, or great, it was gonna take work. Is something that my dad drilled into my head at a very young age. If you're cutter, you're gonna know how to work and you're gonna work everyone else. An academics this man doing all of the homework and doing every bit of extra credit that was available.

Part of my drive was doing better than everyone else, and the other part was doing the best that I can. There were a few times that I almost forgot my homework or something that I was supposed to hand in and I remember feeling physically sick because of it, I hated the feeling of not doing enough.

I do not recall any connection between my physical impairments and an impact on my schoolwork. I had straight A's in every class until my sophomore year in high school. My siblings got paid for their A's and I only got paid for A's in third grade. by my senior year when I had caught a severe case of senioritis, I decided to say F school, F homework, F everything because I was not gonna be living for anyone else's expectations. My parents and the people around me, expected me to get straight A's.
