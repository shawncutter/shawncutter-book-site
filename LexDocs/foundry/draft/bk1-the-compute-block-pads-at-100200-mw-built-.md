# The Compute Block (Pads at 100–200 MW, Built to Iterate)

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/68342e92-4ce2-4b20-87cf-c925c61ef64e-RackMultipart20251015-170-rru9i7.jpeg)

**Forging**

We start **pad‑level** — **100 to 200 MW** —because hyperscale doesn’t audition; it moves in. Each pad is an **iteration machine** : fast to stand up, faster to adapt, and graceful to retire or redeploy.

**What a pad includes**

- **Liquid‑ready backbones.** Dual coolant loops sized to **80–120 kW/rack** with headroom, rear‑door heat exchangers, and aisle containment that can flip with the next GPU class.

- **N+1 power and cooling** with dual paths (behind‑the‑meter + grid interop).

- **Lift‑and‑shift design.** Racks, power skids, and even sections of bus and pipe are **removable modules** with standard flanges and connectors.

- **Circular compute.** We design for a **second‑hand market** from day one: clear acceptance tests, certified wipe & refurb processes, logistics for redeploying older gear to smaller campuses, municipalities, or training centers.

**Why this matters**

- **Iteration is the product.** Hyperscale performance curves won’t wait. Pads that **refresh without rewiring** keep revenue and credibility.

- **Residual value is a lever.** A healthy secondary market reduces risk and waste, and keeps partners loyal because last year’s gear still has a home.

> **Rule:** _Build big, but build for iteration—and design the second‑hand market into the pad._

**AI Playbook — Iterate‑Ready Campus**

**Purpose:** Make 100–200 MW pads fast to deploy and faster to refresh. **Prompt:**

1. **Interfaces:** Lock electrical/fluid/mechanical flanges and quick‑connects for racks and skids; publish a **no‑bespoke** list.

2. **Refresh Plan:** Define the 24‑month rolling **lift‑and‑shift** (acceptance tests, wipe/refurb workflow, transport specs).

3. **Thermal Envelope:** Set winter/summer rack envelopes and validation steps in the twin.

4. **Secondary Market:** Pre‑qualify buyers/recipients (edge sites, EDU, civic compute) and write contracts in advance.

5. **KPI:** Time‑to‑pad‑ready; time‑to‑refresh; % of gear reused/redeployed; residual value curve. **Constraints:**

- Commissioning must pass **offline/low‑bandwidth** tests.

- No new connectors in Pad 1.

- Every pad ships with **heat/CO₂ reuse stubs**.
