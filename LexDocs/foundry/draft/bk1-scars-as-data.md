# Scars as Data

## How injury became my operating system

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/5582e94d-82e6-4177-9f49-57ba9c50b75d-RackMultipart20251130-141-efzyvh.png)

After a shower I’ll catch myself tracing the old line on my thigh. Pins below bone. Stories below skin. The scar doesn’t hurt much anymore. It just sits there, quiet, until a decision lands that rhymes with whatever caused it.

Same with my shoulder. There’s a twinge I get before big hires or big raises. For a long time, I didn’t connect those. I just thought, “Weird, must’ve slept wrong.”

Then one day we were prepping to bring on a senior exec. That exact ache flared. Déjà vu, but in my body. I paused. Looked back. Realized: every time I’d **ignored** that feeling in the past, I’d rushed a relationship that later blew up.

I stopped treating it like superstition. I wrote it down.

### When the body speaks before the brain

A few months into logging, my shoulder lit up the day before a VP interview.

Old pattern: ignore it, enjoy the chemistry, “I’ll backfill the diligence later.” New pattern: stop, pull the Scar Map.

We slowed the process:

- Added one more reference.

- Asked one harder question in the panel.

- Pressed on the exact area past experience told me was weak.

The reference call saved us a year.

They weren’t evil. They just had a pattern we didn’t have time to rehabilitate. Old me would’ve called that “bad luck” 12 months later. Scar-reading me called it “preventable.”

### Unread scars become superstition

Scars don’t ask for attention. They just quietly change how you compensate:

- You avoid people who look like the one who hurt you, even if they’re not that person.

- You over-control a process because it blew up once, even if conditions changed.

- You distrust ease because the last time something felt easy, you missed a risk.

If you don’t **read** your scars, the compensation ossifies into doctrine:

- “Always move fast.”

- “Never trust X type of person.”

- “Only hire from Y background.”

- “Investors are all snakes.”

- “Employees will always leave.”

You start avoiding risk that isn’t actually risky and walking straight past danger that is.

Unread scars turn into superstition and prejudice. Read scars turn into **strategy**.

### At EnergiAcres and Terrasow: scars → playbooks

We started baking scars into the operating system.

Examples:

- **Budget tripwires**

- **Hiring fuses**

- **Brownout protocols**

- **Founder fuses**

This isn’t therapy cosplay. It’s fieldcraft.

In a shop, when a weld cracks, you don’t do a TED Talk about it. You:

- Grind it out.

- Prep the joint better.

- Adjust heat and speed.

- Log what went wrong so the next weld holds.

Scars are that grind mark. They’re not curses. They’re instructions.

### Personal Scar Map examples (outside of money)

Not all scars are balance-sheet ones.

- **Family scar:** Abrupt, chaotic communication around crises.

- **Health scar:** Ignoring early fatigue until my body forced a hard stop.

- **Reputation scar:** One public failure that made me overly cautious on the next big bet.

Scars are context. They’re not destiny.
