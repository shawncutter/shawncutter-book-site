# Removing the Blockers (Location, Location, Location)

## Speed isn’t a mystery; it’s a map.

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/e07e8ec9-08b2-4b62-95b7-0ca68c58bf7b-RackMultipart20251015-134-jg72fb.jpeg)

Speed isn’t a mystery; it’s a **map**. Put a campus **near power, gas, fiber, water, sewer, roads, rail, and workforce** —and in a **community that wants to win** —and the blockers melt.

### **The checklist that makes everything go faster**

- **Power & Gas:** Tap paths and substation siting are visible **from the pad.**

- **Fiber:** Dual‑path long‑haul with meet‑me conduits built into the civil plan.

- **Water & Sewer:** Intake, discharge, and treatment planned with **reuse** in mind.

- **Workforce:** Training partners lined up (trades + ops) with paid pipelines.

- **Civic Fit:** Zoning path, pre‑apps, incentives; neighbors briefed early; **public KPIs** promised.

- **Relationship OS:** EnergiAcres runs the daily handshake between **city, utility, midstream, EPC, and community** —the governance that turns “maybe” into “moving.”

> **Rule:** _Pick the right location and half the blockers never show up._

## Locking the Path (Permits, People, Paper) 

### Speed loves certainty. Lock the steps that trigger real work: site control, permits, and who decides what when.

- **Site Control:** LOI → PSA → recorded MOU with the city. Everyone knows the clock.

- **Permits Matrix:** Full map: zoning, air, water, wetlands, rail, road cuts, utility taps. Owner on the hook for dates.

- **Pre-Apps:** One table. City, county, utility, midstream, DOT, EPA reps. Questions answered live.

- **Environmental:** Phase I/II done early. Wetland and cultural flags cleared or redesigned out.

- **Title/ALTA:** No surprises at foundation. Encroachments fixed in design, not in the field.

- **Decision RACI:** One accountable owner per decision. SLA on decisions: 48–72 hours or escalate.

> Rule: If it’s not on the matrix, it’s a rumor.

## Capital Choreography (Money That Moves Dirt)

### Capital speed equals project speed. Tie money to milestones so work never idles.

- **Stack Map:** Equity, debt, incentives, grants—one page, staged by milestone.

- **Draw Cadence:** Weekly pay-apps. Net-15 or better. Subs don’t float your dream.

- **Risk Budget:** 10–15% contingency, owner-held. Use it like a scalpel, not a blanket.

- **Term Discipline:** Covenants that honor construction reality. No nonsense triggers.

- **Local Match:** Align public dollars to public KPIs. Jobs, training seats, vendor growth.

> Rule: If funding isn’t milestone-bound, it’s calendar-bound—and calendars kill speed.

## Operational Drumbeat (Cadence Over Chaos)

### Chaos is the tax. Cadence is the refund.

- **Daily:** 15-minute stand-up. Safety, blockers, next actions. No storytelling.

- **Weekly:** Critical path review. Red/yellow flags with owners and dates.

- **Source of Truth:** One map, one schedule, one issue log. No private spreadsheets.

- **Owner’s Rep:** One neck to grab. Authority to decide, not just observe.

- **Change Control:** Scope shifts with price/time deltas approved same day.

> Rule: No heroes—systems win.

## Design for Failure (Spares, Switchovers, Scenarios) 

### Assume something breaks. Make sure nothing stops.

- **Redundancy:** N+1 on what matters; hot switchover tested, not promised.

- **Black-Start:** Playbook printed and drilled. People know the first three moves.

- **Spares:** On-site kit for the long-lead parts that fail at 2 a.m.

- **Vendors:** SLAs with teeth. Escalation trees taped to the wall.

- **Tabletop Drills:** Quarterly “what if” reps. Fire, flood, outage, protest.

> Rule: Scars are data—codify the lesson the same day.

## Governance That Moves (Fast, Final, Documented) 

### Speed is a decision habit.

- **SLA on Answers:** 24–72 hours by tier. Silence is a “no” you can escalate.

- **Decision Log:** Who decided, what, why, impact. Shared and searchable.

- **Stop-the-Meeting Test:** If it doesn’t change next week’s work, it’s an email.

- **Risk Queue:** Top five risks reviewed weekly with named owners.

> Rule: Radical transparency beats radical optimism.

## The 90-Day Run (From Dirt to Doing) 

### Compress uncertainty into short, violent sprints.

- Days 0–30: Site control, permits matrix, utility pre-apps, survey/ALTA, geotech. Drumbeat starts.

- Days 31–60: Civil plan locked, tap paths awarded, long-lead POs placed, workforce MOUs signed.

- Days 61–90: Ground broken, telemetry boxes live, draw cadence stable, community KPIs published.

> Rule: Measure twice, trench once.

## Relationship OS (The Daily Handshake) 

### EnergiAcres runs the handshake that turns “maybe” into “moving.”

- Utility + Midstream: Weekly joint plan. Outage windows, tie-ins, and crews aligned.

- City + County: One page of asks, one page of gives. Updated live.

- EPC + Community: Work windows promised; jobs posted; complaints closed.

- Investors: Milestone-based updates with cash and risk deltas in plain language.

> Rule: Connect the dots. Innovation lives in the seams.

## **AI Playbook — Location Fit Score (0–100)** 

Use this when you need a fast, apples‑to‑apples read on any site—AI compute, manufacturing, logistics, renewables, or civic infrastructure. In under 60 minutes (public maps + first‑call inputs), it converts universal siting realities into a single 0–100 “location fit” score. Categories are sector‑agnostic (proximity, jurisdiction readiness, community compact, execution risk), and the pass/fail gates tell you whether to advance diligence or archive with notes on what would have changed the score.

**Purpose:** Create a quick, sector‑agnostic triage so speed is baked in, not begged for.

**Prompt:**

1. **Proximity Scores (0–10 each):** gas, substation, dual‑path fiber, water/sewer, roads/rail, workforce.

2. **Jurisdiction Readiness (0–20):** pre‑apps, zoning path, incentive stance, permitting culture.

3. **Community Compact (0–20):** civic support, resilience plan (RSL loads), public KPI appetite.

4. **Risk Ledger (0–20):** ROW conflicts, long‑lead items, environmental red flags, neighborhood constraints.

5. **Decision:** Sites ≥80 advance to twin underwriting; \<60 get archived with what would have changed the score. **Constraints:**

- No advance without dual‑path fiber **and** gas tap feasibility.

- Heat/CO₂ reuse stubbed in civil from day one.

- A named “relationship owner” for each counterpart (city, utility, midstream).
