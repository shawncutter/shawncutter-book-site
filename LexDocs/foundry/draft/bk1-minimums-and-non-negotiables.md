# Minimums and Non-Negotiables

## Guardrails that keep your life from resenting your mission 

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/a15a99eb-eeb0-4a69-8ca5-64218858f737-RackMultipart20251130-150-zjxa3p.png)

**Excerpt:** Cedar Valley Farms showed me this: if I’m not slept, trained, and clear, I’m dangerous. Minimums aren’t selfish—they’re the only way I can show up without burning everyone down.

During peak Cedar Valley Farms stress, my days looked like this: wake up tired, slam caffeine, sprint, triage fires, crash late, scroll, repeat. My Whoop, Oura, Apple—every metric screamed red. I treated my body like a generator you can just over-rev forever.

Shock: you can’t.

I noticed something simple and ugly: the worse I treated myself, the worse I treated everyone else. Short sleep = short fuse. No movement = no patience. Garbage food = garbage decisions. Under that load, I wasn’t a leader. I was a hazard with a title.

The farm taught me maintenance on machines. Therapy taught me maintenance on me. Cedar Valley Farms forced me to take it seriously.

So I wrote **minimums and non-negotiables** that had nothing to do with revenue and everything to do with making sure the person driving the tractor wasn’t drunk on stress.

### The body minimums

- **Sleep floor:** 7 hours in bed. I don’t book 5 a.m. calls anymore. If I have to get up early, I go to bed like it matters—because it does.

- **Movement:** 10k steps or 45 minutes of real effort. Lift, walk, ride, something. Every day. No “I was too busy” pass.

- **Food:** No sugar bombs when I’m already cooked. Protein first, then whatever. Not performance nutrition perfection. Just not sabotage.

- **Alcohol:** Hard limit. I don’t blunt the edges I’m supposed to be reading.

These became non-negotiable. Not goals. Standards. I keep them like I keep a safety protocol on a rig.

### The time minimums

- **Four dinners a week.** Seven exist. I’m in a chair for four, phone away. No excuses, just re-prioritization.

- **Bridge Hour daily.** Sixty minutes where I’m not CEO, founder, or fixer. Just Dad/husband/human.

- **One off-grid day a week.** Sunday. The world survives. The inbox can wait.

When I miss, I don’t pretend. We run a **brownout protocol** : I drop one noncritical initiative for seven days and win back the basics.

### Why this isn’t selfish

People who don’t like boundaries will call this selfish. They’re wrong.

When I honor minimums:

- I don’t snap at my kids for being kids.

- I don’t turn every meeting into a referendum on my worth.

- I don’t try to brute-force Cedar Valley Farms or EnergiAcres through sheer cortisol.

- I see problems earlier. I course-correct quieter.

And people notice. They see me training, walking, shutting the laptop at dinner. Some copy it. Some roll their eyes. Doesn’t matter. The standard is set. Culture is caught, not taught.

If I can’t defend 7 hours of sleep, 45 minutes of movement, and one hour of presence, I don’t deserve to talk about defending “the mission.”

**Cutter Rule:** Minimums aren’t indulgence. They’re the cost of not turning into the problem. **Field Test:** Write three body minimums and three time minimums. Put them where you see them daily. Track for 14 days. When you break one twice, drop one noncritical project for a week.
