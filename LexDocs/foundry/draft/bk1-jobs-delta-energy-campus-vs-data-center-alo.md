# Jobs Delta: Energy Campus vs. Data Center Alone

## The workforce math that actually matters.

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/3841a8c2-71b3-469f-8e90-0165c880fb68-RackMultipart20251130-150-am84fw.png)

Scene: You’re at a diner across from the mayor. Coffee rings on the Formica. Between you is a napkin with two columns:

- Column A: **“Hyperscale DC only.”**

- Column B: **“Energy + Data + Food Campus.”**

Under each, a rough stack of jobs per MW. The difference is not subtle.

### The problem with MW-only thinking

The industry loves to measure by megawatts.

- 100 MW here.

- 200 MW there.

- “Tens of thousands of jobs” in the press release that quietly turn into a few hundred ongoing roles once the construction teams go home.

A hyperscale data center alone is brutally efficient on headcount:

- Incredible when you care about margin.

- Terrible if you’re a city betting on it as your primary jobs engine.

You get:

- A spike of construction work.

- Then a long, thin tail of operations roles—good jobs, but not many of them.

Our whole thesis with EnergiAcres is different: **energy campus first, compute second** , with food and fabrication wired into the same stack.

That changes the workforce math.

### The energy campus stack

When you anchor everything on an **energy-first ecosystem** , you don’t just get a building that hums quietly. You get layers of work that don’t exist in a pure DC-only play:

1. **24/7 energy operations**

2. **Greenhouse and food systems**

3. **Fabrication and maintenance**

4. **Training cohorts**

5. **Local compute help desks and edge ops**

Same MW, very different human footprint.

### “Jobs Delta” in practice

The **Jobs Delta** is simply:

> **Energy Campus Jobs − Data Center-Only Jobs per MW**

### Locals first—paid seats, not promises

“Local jobs” is another over-used phrase that often means “we will post them on a website and hope you see them.”

We’re taking a different approach:

- **Job ladder published before ground breaks**

- **Trainees on payroll**

- **Scholarships and tool grants tied to start dates**

“Locals first” only means something if the gates are clear, the seats are real, and people can pay rent while they ramp.

### How we measure the jobs delta

The KPI skeleton for this is straightforward:

- **Jobs/MW by function**

- **Trainees on payroll**

- **Retention at 90/180/365 days**

- **Vendor spend % local**

If you want to be taken seriously by a city that’s been burned before, you show this math in daylight.

### Rule: Publish the ladder before you pour the pad

The discipline here is simple:

> **Rule: Publish the job ladder before you break ground.**

If you can’t show how people climb from:

- Interested → Trainee → Operator → Supervisor → Specialist,

you’re just spraying numbers for headlines.

### Mini Playbook: How to make the jobs delta real

1. **Define roles by shift**

2. **Tie scholarships and tool grants to start dates**

3. **Post retention quarterly**

That’s how you prove the campus is more than a marketing line.
