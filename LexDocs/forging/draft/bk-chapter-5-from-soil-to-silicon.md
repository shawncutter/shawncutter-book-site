# **BK: Chapter 5: From Soil to Silicon**

My real start with computers started with an NECI386, that came loaded with a 200 MB hard drive that we were supposed to never be able to fill. We now routinely build computers with several terabytes of storage. the computer was loaded up at DOS and we utilized Lotus notes and other DOS-based applications.

The computer was purchased by cutter oil company and partly justified because my mom also did the bookkeeping for another construction company nearby called Palmer and son excavating. this computer would help digitally track in compute oil and gas royalties and distributions.

This computer was also my first playground and entrance to the Internet with a dial up modem that was 2400 baud and also introduced me to video games. What I found most interesting where the games that allowed for customization and configuration of the game experience allowing me to create my own worlds. I was very into math games that measured both accuracy and speed.

There were times during the summer when I was stuck in the house because I was on crutches in the computer was one hell of a way to pastime.

From that point on I was hooked and I would be directly involved in any other technology purchased by the family because I was a little obsessed. The first I set up was in our house. the first ISDN line that I created was at our house which was an interesting thing to do because we had multiple phone lines and so I figured out a way with multiple modems, we could double the speed of dial up. This was super important because back then AOL was just starting to send out CDs in the mail.

I was an MIS major at Miami University and chose this major because it offered a path to gain experience with the newly minted [microsoft.net](http://microsoft.net). Microsoft was strongly competing with Java at the time, and I made a conscious decision to focus my skills and professional life on the Microsoft stack rather than open source. I realize that it was going to be very hard to become great working on open source and Microsoft at the same time and chose Microsoft because they had much more professional ID experiences, and I saw the link between the experience and the productivity that I could gain with the proper tools.

As I narrowed my focus into technology during myology years, I felt strongly that my place in the world was hardware and networking. I worked hard and received an MCSE, and MCDBA, a CCNA, and many other Microsoft certifications. however since I graduated college at the end of the.com bust, there were no jobs for heavily certified recent college grads with no full-time real world experience outside of side hustles and projects I created.

I was living in Michigan at the time with my girlfriend who is in law school at Michigan State, and we had plans that I would find a job and live with her and work from that location. But I would soon find out how difficult it was finding a in limited markets with skills, but a lack of experience. I drove all over the state of Michigan performing consulting work, such as setting up windows, machines, and networks and active directory for companies. eventually, I had to face the music and started doing job searches near my hometown with the idea that I may have to move back to live with my parents and save money.

I eventually got a job which was a technology Company that served a large radiology group in Northeast Ohio spanning 17 hospitals. This was a networking and software job and that we had VPN and connections to all of those hospitals and had to connect to and extract all patients and procedures performed. within one first time I was running the department and designing entirely new applications that serve the company for years. This cemented my interest in software and the ability to craft technology that changed human workflows for the better in the mid 2000s we had radiologist performing their work from Florida, serving patients in Ohio.

I also learned to build software that game of the experience, associating bonus to medical coders for both speed and accuracy. Witness first-hand incentive added into the application could have a big impact on the results.

The goal was always to move to Florida although I did not tell that to my first employer in Ohio until the time was right. I ended up sticking with big thumb working on the side, even though I had moved to Florida and got a full-time job at an early company serving the multilevel marketing industry. there are good companies that have a multilevel business model, but most of these companies are scams built by those who are looking for easy money, leveraging their existing downlines moving from scheme to new scheme. I was hired at that job because of my networking and hardware experience in that was partly to justify the salary requirements I had at the time and that I had to help manage the servers at the colo, set up networks in the office, and also modernize their software stack to [asp.net](http://asp.net).

I worked by design technologies for a year and at my one year anniversary I asked for a raise. This story demonstrates my focus on personal development and ability to set goals and make tough decisions that align to my long-term vision. I started with a salary of $60,000 and at one year I wanted to be making $75,000. When I told the owner and boss of the company this of what I wanted and made it clear that this was a request and that I was willing to do even more work in order to meet that. The owner wanted to take me up to $65,000 and what I explained to him that he didn't understand my request and that I wanted to make $75,000 and it was up to him to come up with whatever else that I had to do in order to achieve that salary. The boss came back the next day and offered me $70,000 and I knew at that moment that I would not be working there any longer and I had to start looking for a job later.

A week later when I talk to the boss and told him I would be moving on he offered me $120,000 a year to stay. This far exceeded the goals that I had said, but I knew that if I stayed that my job was probably at risk because he was not happy with having to pay that amount of money. two weeks later I was working at Talon and I will go on to make $130,000 that next year. I said a personal goal to myself that I would work my ass off in order to exceed that hundred and $20,000 a year that way I knew I had made the right decision.

Joining Tallan was one of the best decisions I made in that it opened up many doors, friendships, and a mountain of learnings that are irreplaceable. Some of my best friendships that I have are still maintained today. Was an early investor and contributor to my first company, and I would not have been successful without them.

TalLan was interested in that they paid their consultants for extra time worked beyond the expectation for the project and for achieving certifications, something I was good at. In two years, I had amassed another eight certifications from Microsoft, this was both good for the company and good for myself. Yes I learned how to study and test, but I also use these certifications as a way to learn the material which I believe is the goal in the first place.
