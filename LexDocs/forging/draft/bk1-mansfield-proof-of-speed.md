# Mansfield: Proof of Speed

## We Don’t Guess

## Forging Infrastructure

We don’t pick sites by hope. We underwrite them the way you’d underwrite a city—because that’s what an AI factory is: a city-sized load parked on one spot.

Every wire, molecule, and worker matters.

We take the full terrain—gas corridors, substation headroom, feeder topology, fiber trunks, water rights, soils, wetlands, logistics, workforce bands—and build the digital twin before we even call a surveyor.

Then we stress it like a living organism:

- What do the feeders feel when we add a city-scale load?

- How much gas can we _actually_ flow with today’s taps, and which lateral earns the right to feed it first?

- Where do we drop pads so thermal loops run short and straight, not long and loud?

- What rack densities can the liquid loop carry in February mud vs. August heat?

- How do we keep cold storage and greenhouses in the slipstream of compute—without tripping over zoning or permits?

We work backward from **energy** —for compute, for power, for crops, for cold—and then forward through construction and commissioning. It’s not a slide deck. It’s a simulation. **Proof of work. Proof of value. Proof of power.**

And we’re doing it in **Mansfield, Ohio.**

This is the first real site where the loops close fast enough to matter.

### **Why Mansfield?**

Because it’s _not_ Silicon Valley, and that’s the point. It’s industrial muscle with room to think. It’s a place where you can pull gas, power, water, and people within five miles. It’s where heat and headcount both stay local.

We chose it because the digital twin said “yes” before anyone else did.

The AI didn’t just render pretty maps—it stress-tested every constraint we could throw:

- a 300-MW ramp in a 1950s grid,

- a gas lateral that has to feed both turbines and greenhouses,

- a workforce that can build racks, drive forklifts, and grow food under glass.

Every loop has to pay its own way—thermally, economically, socially. Mansfield passed.

> **Rule:** _Underwrite with the twin; build from energy backward._

### **AI Playbook — Energy‑Back Underwrite (Mansfield Pattern)**

**Purpose:** Prove a site in software before it moves a shovel. **Prompt:**

1. **Data Load:** Gas (pressure, ROW), grid (substation/feeder), fiber (dual‑path), water (intake/discharge), soils, logistics, workforce.

2. **Scenarios:** City‑size load phases (100→300→500 MW), winter/summer extremes, outage drills, construction sequencing stress.

3. **Results:** Pad placement, one‑lines, thermal/CO₂ routing, feeder upgrades, **district thermal** stubs, traffic & construction phasing.

4. **Risks:** ROW conflicts, permit gates, long‑lead equipment, neighborhood impacts—with mitigations and owners.

5. **Decision Doc:** A go/no‑go with a budget range, schedule, and public **resilience KPIs**. **Constraints:**

- No “go” without dual‑path fiber, gas with demonstrated volumetrics, and a grid interop plan.

- Heat/CO₂ reuse stubs day one.

- Public dashboard readiness at Gate‑1.
