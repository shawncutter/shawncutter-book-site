# Fractured → Forging - The Forty Percent Ask

## Family, equity, and the boundary that saved my company.

I was in a rental car in Hartford, Connecticut, headed back to a hotel after a consulting day. Bluetooth crackle. CJ’s voice: _“I want forty percent. It was my idea. You’re building me a tool. I’ll help sell it.”_

I stared at the highway lines and said what was true: _“There’s no way that can work. I have to give equity to investors. I’d lose majority control in the first round.”_ He didn’t understand what I was saying. I understand why now.

Here’s what equity taught me the hard way: most people treat it like gratitude in percentages. It’s not. Equity is a **to‑do list with a timer**. If the work doesn’t show, the promise rots. CJ did invest. He made money back. Resentment still leaked around the edges—the kind that eats holidays and phone calls.

It wasn’t just family. My CTO and SVP of Sales got equity to come up and build Fielding Systems. Only one of them valued it. The other treated it like confetti—nice in the air, nothing in the hand. Equity kept plaguing my life because it was built on hope and proximity, not on proof.

So I killed the idea tax. I wrote a boundary and taped it above my monitor: **No “idea tax.” Equity is earned by delivered work, not claimed by proximity.** Cliffs. Milestones. Vesting that doesn’t blink. Clear roles. I stopped letting love and guilt run the cap table. I let reality and contribution do it.

Today I’m codifying that boundary with **Earned‑Value Shares (EVS)**—a programmable class that accrues to people who _prove_ value. Smart contracts track the milestones; governance keeps it honest. It’s still human. It’s just harder to lie.

I still love my brother. Family is complicated and true. The company survived because I chose a boundary over a bruise. Turns out that’s leadership, too.

**One‑line rule:** _Equity is not a thank‑you—it's an operating agreement with receipts._

**Constraints**

- Include one measurable milestone (e.g., “10 qualified sales by Q2”).

- Plain English: who does what by when.

- Make the other person human—no villains.
