# **Jobs Delta: Energy Campus vs. Pure Data Center** _The workforce math that actually matters._

Bacon grease, burnt coffee, laminated menu. The mayor slides into the booth. I pull a pen and flatten a napkin. Two columns. “Data center” on the left. “Energy campus” on the right. We don’t talk sizzle. We talk seats.

The left side is lean by design. A hyperscale DC runs quietly—security, facilities, a skeleton crew on shifts. I write a number that makes people frown: around one head per MW once it settles. [NEEDS CHECK] High wages, low count. Good tax base. Not much traffic.

On the right side, I stack the ladder. 24/7 operations for power and heat. Greenhouse crews are on two shifts. Maintenance/fab bay. Training cohort paid to learn. Local compute help desk for small businesses that can’t wait for a cloud ticket. I write four to seven heads per MW across functions. [NEEDS CHECK] The mayor watches the columns fill. The coffee cools. This is the turn—jobs per MW move when you layer power + food + compute.

We don’t make promises we can’t staff. We publish the ladder: roles, pay bands, shifts, training timelines, and local‑first seats. Scholarships and tool grants tied to start dates. Retention posted at 90/180/365 days. No heroes—systems win. If we can’t keep people, the design is wrong.

Farmer cadence: chores with names, times, and weather plans. Oilfield cadence: callout lists, spares, stand‑ups that end on time. Startup cadence: ship weekly, debrief the miss, adjust the loop. EnergiAcres cadence: all three, plus a greenhouse that breathes out warmth at 2 a.m.

We shake hands over the napkin. “Publish this,” he says. “Before you break ground.” We will. Jobs aren’t a headline. They’re a roster.

**AI tie.** AI is the shift boss that never sleeps. It builds coverage maps from outages and weather, writes 90‑day curricula from SOPs, and pairs apprentices to mentors based on tasks, not vibes. It watches churn in real time and flags where the ladder is missing a rung. AI as coach, not king.

**Rule: Publish the job ladder before you break ground.**
