# This Starts Here: A Gen‑X Technologist, Energy‑Farming Nerd, and an AI

_I’ve been early to everything except telling the truth about my own story. That changes today._

I grew up between row crops and rotary phones, between diesel and dial‑up. I’m Gen X—raised on chores and static, the last analog generation to become fluent in code. I’ve been an early adopter my whole life: first kid to ride the new machine, first one playing Doom on the T1 at school, first founder to push oilfield to the cloud and mobile. I built, I shipped, I exited. Then I did it again. And then I mistook motion for momentum. I said yes to everything, spread thin across machines and meetings, until the plates spun me instead of the other way around. I almost broke—of energy, cash, body, marriage, faith—hairline cracks everywhere. The fix wasn’t speed; it was truth. Tell it, simplify, choose fewer, go deeper. EnergiAcres and Terrasow are what happened when I stopped hiding behind roadmaps and spreadsheets and picked the work that could carry weight for decades—energy, software, food—wired together to hum. Perfection is just fear in a nicer suit. Consistency has been my white whale. It’s time to write what actually happened, while I’m still close enough to smell the diesel on it. It happens here, in public, on Medium.

## What this series is

Short stories. Stand‑alone scenes. No mythology. No sanitized founder narrative. Just one memory at a time—farm, accident, startups, exits, rebuilding, faith lost, purpose found—each one ending with the practical thing it taught me about building systems, companies, and a life that doesn’t pass pain forward. Think field notes from a life spent between mud and motherboards.

## Why Medium

Because shipping beats stewing. Because public accountability makes cowardice expensive. Because the editor is a blinking cursor and a publish button. The goal isn’t virality; it’s repetition. Write, ship, repeat until the shape of the book appears—Forged, Fractured, Forging, Foundry—one honest scene at a time.

## My co‑author is an AI

I work with AI the way I work a farm or a power plant: inputs, flows, feedback loops. I’ll show you exactly how I use it—prompts, constraints, revision passes, and the “good enough to ship” threshold. This isn’t AI as parlor trick; it’s a power tool. I use it to structure chaos, to ask harder questions than I would alone, and to keep me honest when my memory tries to round off the sharp edges. If you’ve got a company in your head or a story in your chest, I want you to see—in public, step‑by‑step—how to unlock it with the same tools. As Taleb put it, “Wind extinguishes a candle and energizes fire.” I’m choosing fire.

## What you’ll get in every story

- A scene you can feel (sound, smell, texture—the way memory actually works)

- A principle I still use to lead and build (systems over heroics)

- An AI Playbook sidebar with the exact pattern I used (prompt, constraint, outcome)

- A question to turn the mirror toward you (so you can build, too)

## Where this is headed

I’ll move between three lanes and keep the cadence tight:

- **Energy** — upstream grit, midstream control systems, downstream discipline; how those flows trained me to think in cause‑and‑effect and design for failure.

- **Tech** — from LAN parties and packet sniffers to cloud platforms and now AI agents; how abstraction gets dangerous if you forget the dirt underneath.

- **Food/Farming** — the truth serum for every big idea; if it can’t survive mud, weather, and time, it doesn’t work. EnergiAcres lives at that intersection. Terrasow does, too.

## My promise

I will not glorify pain. I will not hide behind jargon. I will not pretend consistency is easy. I’ll show up anyway. If a story isn’t ready, I’ll post a smaller one. If a scene scares me, I’ll publish it first. The goal is momentum, not mythology.

You can drop in anywhere, but if you want a path, follow the arcs as they emerge. _Forged_ will come first—farm, accident, work as camouflage. _Fractured_ will tell the truth about losing faith and finding questions. _Forging_ will build systems from the rubble—startups, exits, EnergiAcres and Terrasow. _Foundry_ will name the philosophy I actually live by now: resilience with receipts.
