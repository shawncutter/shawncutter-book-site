# The Microgrid Is a Neighborhood (Mansfield Vision)

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/7dd4e261-7451-4ece-bd73-bd77147d5076-RackMultipart20251015-170-p6hah2.jpeg)

Rain ran sideways and the street blinked. The greenhouse kept its own weather, steady and warm. Two doors down, the **Neighbor Circuit** outlet glowed red—quiet proof that someone had planned for the tired hour.

That’s the small picture. The big picture is **Mansfield reimagined** : not just one house that doesn’t go dark, but a **city that doesn’t go dark.** No rolling blackouts. No rolling brownouts. A network that fails gracefully because it is **designed** to carry people, not just loads.

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/df648c0d-04c4-4d51-9f29-8a2adcad234c-RackMultipart20251015-170-gv5lp7.jpeg)

**What the Neighborhood Circuit becomes at city scale**

- **Anchor + Orchestra.** A behind‑the‑meter campus (our energy park) serves as an **anchor plant** and orchestration brain. It supplies compute, captures heat/CO₂, and—when the grid stumbles—becomes a **polite neighbor** that stabilizes rather than spikes.

- **Feeder‑level “Neighbor Circuits.”** Critical loads across town are mapped and ranked: clinics, cold‑chain, water plant, traffic signaling, communications towers, grocery hubs, and designated shelters. Each gets a **resilience service level (RSL)** with time guarantees (e.g., 24–72 hours) under islanded operation.

- **Switching + Sectionalizing.** Automated reclosers and sectionalizers create **micro‑islands** that can be reconfigured in seconds to route power around faults.

- **Distributed Storage & Thermal.** BESS nodes (utility‑side and customer‑side) and **district thermal loops** buffer swings without asking humans to be heroic.

- **Human Loop.** A community call‑tree and **door‑hanger SOPs** bring people into the plan: which outlets to use, which loads to pause, who checks on whom.

**How the system actually behaves**

- **Islanding “with manners.”** When the upstream grid falters, the campus islands with a **sip‑not‑chug** generator cadence, preserving battery **SOC guardrails** (e.g., never below a set floor while RSL loads are active).

- **Priority choreography.** AI schedules noncritical loads down and RSL loads up, with receipts: “Why we did this, for how long, and what you’ll notice.”

- **Black‑start rehearsals.** Monthly drills bring feeders up in a predetermined sequence, verified in the **digital twin** (below) before anyone touches a switch.

**Mansfield, future state**

- The **airport‑adjacent energy park** is the anchor.

- Feeder automation wraps the neighborhoods most likely to suffer first and longest under stress.

- The city, utility, campus, and key employers agree on **“resilience compacts”** that pre‑commit roles, triggers, and communications.

- Quarterly public metrics: avoided outage minutes, RSL performance, food‑miles cut, heat recovered, and **GPU‑hours donated** to schools and workforce.

> **Rule:** _Design power to carry people, not just loads—and design neighborhoods that don’t go dark._

**AI Playbook — Neighborhood Circuit, City‑Scale**

**Purpose:** Convert a campus + town into a coordinated resilience network. **Prompt:**

1. **Critical‑Load Registry:** Inventory loads (clinic cold store, water intake, traffic corridors, comm towers, shelters) and assign **RSL tiers** with time goals.

2. **Feeder Map & Switch Plan:** Identify sectionalizing points, auto‑reclosers, and island boundaries; define a **black‑start order**.

3. **Anchor Coordination:** Specify campus export windows, SOC floors, and “polite neighbor” limits (frequency/voltage droop, ramp rates).

4. **Distributed Buffers:** Place BESS and district thermal extensions where they serve multiple RSL loads.

5. **Human Protocols:** Door‑hanger SOPs, call trees, and a quarterly 30‑minute drill that logs start/stop, SOC delta, exceptions. **Constraints:**

- **Local‑first control** with printed runbooks; cloud optional.

- Any shed that touches RSL loads requires **human acknowledgment**.

- Public KPI board: outage minutes avoided, RSL uptime, SOC compliance, drill score.
