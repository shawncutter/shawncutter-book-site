# Silo Steps, No Handle

_Training fear by exposure you can survive._

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/db1ddbb7-e4e4-42cf-b110-36b279cfb8cc-RackMultipart20251006-188-9jgitd.png)

Metal rung. Cold bite. Silage smell that lives in your clothes. The silo ladder disappeared into a sky the color of tin. Dad on the rungs below. There was no telling him no.

Halfway up, wind started talking. At the top, the ladder curled and the platform offered no friendly grab point. No handle. Reach blind, swing a leg, commit your weight, trust steel.

I did it ugly. Then again. The fear didn’t leave; it shrank until it fit the job. The farm demands the work, not your feelings about the work.

That ladder wrote a rule I use in boardrooms and storm drills: **expose, don’t avoid.** Break the scary thing into rungs. Practice the swing. Add a leash—procedure, checklist, a human override. Courage is reps, not vibes.

**AI tie.** I use AI like a coach. It breaks one fear into five rungs, writes the drill, sets the stop‑loss, and prints the one‑page checklist so “reach‑swing‑commit” is a move, not a gamble.

_Rule: Step into fear in increments you can survive._

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/a273aef5-7a27-4e14-861f-bdb4cd09a1e2-RackMultipart20251006-140-pcrdnq.png)

**AI Playbook — Exposure Ladder Builder**

**Purpose:** Turn fear into a practiced move.

**Prompt:** “Name one fear (room, feature, outage drill). (1) Define the ‘top’ outcome, (2) create 5 rungs of rising difficulty, (3) list success/fail signals per rung, (4) generate a one‑page checklist with a human override each step.”

**Constraints:** Each rung \<30 minutes • Friendly skeptic for top rungs • Log outcomes local‑first.
