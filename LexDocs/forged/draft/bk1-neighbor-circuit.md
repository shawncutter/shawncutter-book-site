# Neighbor Circuit

## Power is personal when the lights go out.

Rain ran sideways, the kind that slaps. The greenhouse hummed steady, fans ticking a beat I trust. Then the street went dark—one long blink, transformer groan, silence. Our fence line held its own weather.

On the tablet, the microgrid slid into island mode: solar tapering, battery stepping in, generator warming like a polite neighbor. Loads reprioritized—fish tanks and seedlings high; office heat and convenience plugs low. One line sat at the top in bold: **Neighbor Circuit**.

We built that line on purpose—a subpanel that fed the elderly couple two doors down, their fridge and a medical device that doesn’t care about storm narratives. I pulled on a jacket and walked over. Their kitchen light was soft. The compressor hummed. She looked at the outlet like it was a message. It was.

Back inside, the AI agent did what it does best— **conduct choreography** , not play hero. It shifted noncritical loads, learned the weather’s mood, scheduled a generator sip instead of a chug, and wrote a one‑minute note on what changed and why. Every suggestion showed receipts: last storm, similar curves, confidence band. I signed off and the system kept its manners.

An hour later, the grid apologized with a flicker. We rejoined gracefully. No speeches, no victory laps. Just a quiet rule confirmed: resilience is measured in people you can help, **not** graphs you can screenshot.

_Rule: Design power to carry people, not just loads._

## **AI Playbook — Neighbor Circuit Sprint**

**Purpose:** Turn your system into a lifeline for the most vulnerable nearby.

**Prompt:** “Map a _neighbor circuit_ for my site. (1) Identify the critical neighbor loads (fridge, med device, heat), (2) propose a subpanel + protected outlets, (3) define islanding priorities and SOC guardrails, (4) generate a call tree + one‑page door‑hanger instruction, (5) schedule a quarterly 30‑minute drill.”

**Constraints:**

- Local‑first control; cloud optional.

- Human override for any load shed affecting neighbors.

- Boring backup (manual cord/transfer) must exist.
