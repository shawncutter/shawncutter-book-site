# The Permit Ledger: Turning “Maybe” into Dates

## If it isn’t dated and owned, it doesn’t exist.

 ![Image](https://lex-img-p.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/img/749f11e2-32ab-4c9e-8008-acd81ea96aea-RackMultipart20251130-171-9coyll.png)

Scene: There’s a coffee ring on the only piece of paper that actually matters this week: a one-page permit matrix.

Three cells are red—due dates this week, owners named. Two cells are yellow—waiting on external agencies. Green stretches down the page where work has cleared.

Everything we’re doing in Mansfield depends on whether that sheet stays mostly green.

### Permitting isn’t mysterious. It’s a queue.

People talk about permitting like it’s weather—unpredictable, uncontrollable, something that just “happens to” projects.

That’s lazy.

Permitting is:

- A **finite** set of approvals.

- In a **finite** sequence.

- Moving at **finite** speeds through a **finite** number of desks.

The problem is rarely “regulation in general.” The problem is:

- Nobody owns the whole picture.

- Dates are fuzzy or missing.

- Blockers are invisible until it’s too late.

We learned this the hard way: _“in review”_ is not a status. It’s a stall.

So we built a **Permit Ledger**.

### From stacks of PDFs to a single ledger

Here’s the idea:

> **If a permit, approval, or coordination step isn’t in the ledger with an owner and a date, it doesn’t exist.**

The ledger is not a thousand-line spreadsheet hidden in someone’s email. It’s:

- One page (plus drill-downs behind it).

- Owned by someone with authority to escalate.

- Updated weekly and published.

Each row has:

- Name of the item (e.g., “State 401/404”, “Traffic Study Sign-off,” “Utility Easement X”).

- Agency or counterparty.

- Current status (RYG is enough).

- Owner on our side.

- Target date and drop-dead date.

- Blockers and next action.

Silence for more than 72 hours on a needed answer automatically triggers escalation.

### How we measure permitting health

Our KPI skeleton:

- **% of permit items on-time**

- **Average days slipped per item**

- **# of escalations resolved inside 72 hours**

- **Public page views**

If we can’t show progress, we don’t deserve patience.

### Rule: Make the ledger public and the excuses vanish.

Once you put the ledger (or at least a public-safe slice) in daylight:

- Vague statements like “it’s stuck in permitting” stop being acceptable.

- Agencies that move fast get visible credit.

- Agencies that drag feet have to explain why in a world where everyone can see the status.

> **Rule: Make the ledger public and the excuses vanish.**

### Mini Playbook: Building your Permit Ledger

1. **Weekly publish**

2. **Link blockers to drawings and docs**

3. **Show resolved and aging items**

If you want to move fast without cutting corners, treat permitting like ops and let the town see the scoreboard.
