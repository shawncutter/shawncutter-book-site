# Password Cracker Crash

## _Curiosity without guardrails breaks more than rules._

Ozone and hot plastic. The lab’s network went from lazy to frantic in a minute. I’d set a password‑cracking tool loose to see if I could “teach myself security.” It started hammering. The school’s network sagged like a fence cut in three places. Teachers lost their gradebooks. Somebody swore down the hall.

I wasn’t evil. I was careless. Curiosity without a fence is a wind that loves fires.

We owned it. I walked to the person in charge and explained what I’d done. No excuses. I offered to fix what I broke. We got a second shot on the condition we did it right: a sandbox, no real accounts, fake data, separate switch, written rules, signatures.

That day turned me into a builder who likes limits. **Consent and containment** are how you get to keep playing.

_AI tie._ Today I red‑team my own systems with AI, but only inside a legal box. The model generates attack paths, we sign the scope, we log every packet, and we never touch real people. Then we fix what we found, not what we fantasized.

_Rule: Permission first. Fence always._

## **AI Playbook — Red‑Team, Right‑Way**

**Purpose:** Learn by attack without collateral damage.

**Prompt:** “Pick a system to test. (1) Write a signed scope (targets, time, methods), (2) create a cloned sandbox with fake data, (3) have AI generate top 10 attack paths, (4) run and log, (5) write a one‑page fix plan.”

**Constraints:**

- Owner signature required.

- No production users/data.

- Publish the fix timeline.
