# FOUNDRY ARC — Morning Recording Prompts
## Villa in Anguilla. March 2026. Hit record and riff.

**Instructions:** These are designed to pull raw material out of you while you're relaxed, sun-soaked, and honest. Don't write — talk. Record voice memos. Go stream-of-consciousness. I'll turn your recordings into polished drafts you'll be proud to ship.

Each section maps to a chapter in the Foundry arc plan. Do them in order or skip to whatever's burning. Aim for 5–10 minutes per question. Some will give you 2 minutes. Some will give you 30. Both are right.

---

## WARM-UP: SET THE SCENE (Do this first — it becomes "Today")

These ground you in the present moment. They become the raw material for the missing "present tense" story.

1. **Where are you right now?** Describe the villa. What do you see from where you're sitting? What does the air feel like? What sounds are happening? Don't edit — just paint it with your voice.

2. **What did the massage today actually do to you?** Not the muscles — the mind. When someone works on a body that's been through 40 surgeries, what happens? Does your body trust hands? When did it start trusting again?

3. **Your kids and the Anguilla sun.** You said they learned the sun is more brutal than Tampa. Tell me that moment. What did they say? What did their faces look like? Did it remind you of anything from the farm?

4. **"Life is so good."** You said that. When was the last time you could say that and mean it? Is there a version of Shawn from five years ago who wouldn't believe this scene? What would you tell him?

5. **"Incrementally better week by week."** Describe what "incrementally better" looks like with your wife right now. Not the highlight reel — the actual mechanics. What's different this month versus six months ago? What's one small thing that shifted?

## CHAPTER 1: "AFTER THE WIRE" — The Space Between Exits

These questions pull the founding story of EnergiAcres and the post-exit void.

6. **The quiet after the wire hit.** After the Fielding exit — when the money landed and the calendar went blank — what was the first morning like? Walk me through that day. What did you do? What didn't you do?

7. **"Rich and empty."** You wrote those words in one of your pieces. Describe what rich-and-empty felt like in your body. Where did you feel it? What did you try to fill it with first?

8. **When did EnergiAcres first exist as an idea?** Not the LLC filing — the moment in your head. Were you driving? In the shop? On a call? What collided to make food + energy + compute into one thing? Walk me through the napkin moment.

9. **Why couldn't you just retire?** You had the exit. You could've bought a boat and fished. What's the thing inside you that wouldn't let that happen? Is it the same engine that kept you alive through 40 surgeries, or is it something different?

10. **Dad's voice in the silence.** After the exit, when things got quiet, did Chuck's voice get louder or quieter? What was he saying? "What's your philosophy on this?" — did that hit different when there was no company to point at?

---

## CHAPTER 2: "CEDAR VALLEY" — The Family Crucible

These pull the before-and-after of Cedar Valley Farms.

11. **The season it worked.** There was a time when Cedar Valley Farms was good. Paint that for me. Harvest day. Siblings together. The Cutter name feeling like armor. What did that taste like?

12. **The first crack.** When did you first feel it shift from fuel to friction with the family? Was it a meeting? A phone call? A look across the table? What was the moment you thought "we're not reading the same playbook"?

13. **CJ in the Foundry.** Where does your brother sit in this chapter of your life? You've written about him in earlier arcs. What's the relationship now — not the polished version, the real one?

14. **The cost of enforcing standards.** You wrote about pushing accountability at CVF and hearing back "you care more about the business than us." When that landed, what happened in your chest? Did you believe them? Do you now?

15. **What you kept, what you dropped, what you rewrote.** In "Quiet Quit of Legacy" you mentioned three columns but left them empty. Fill them now. Out loud. What parts of "Cutter" did you keep? What did you let die? What did you rewrite into something new?

## CHAPTER 3: "THE BLUEPRINT" — Building EnergiAcres

These turn the technical vision pieces into grounded memoir.

16. **The first time you walked the Mansfield site.** What did you see? What did the dirt smell like? What was the weather? When you looked at that land, what did your body tell you — the same body that grew up on a farm?

17. **Why gas-backed compute — told to your daughter.** Pretend Gia asks you at dinner: "Dad, what are you actually building and why?" Explain it to her. No jargon. No investor deck. Just a dad telling his kid why this matters.

18. **"Location, location, location" — but for a farm kid.** You wrote about removing blockers for site selection. But what does "location" mean to someone who grew up on a fixed piece of dirt in Pennsylvania? How does your farm childhood change how you think about where to build?

19. **The scar that saved a hire.** In "Scars as Data" you mention your shoulder aching before big hires. Tell me the specific story. Who was the person? What did the reference call reveal? How close did you come to making that mistake?

20. **The moment you knew the energy campus model was right.** Not intellectually — in your gut. Was there a meeting, a conversation, a number that landed and you thought "this is going to work"? What was the evidence that crossed the line from thesis to conviction?

## CHAPTER 4: "THE MACHINE" — Execution & Discipline

These pull the day-to-day reality of building.

21. **A day in the trailer.** Walk me through an actual work day on site. Morning to night. What does it smell like? Who's there? What's on the whiteboard? What breaks? What holds?

22. **The Whoop/Oura moment.** You mentioned every metric screaming red during CVF stress. Tell me about reading those numbers. What does it feel like to see your own body telling you to stop while your brain says keep going? When did you finally listen?

23. **"Speed is a system."** You wrote about utilities and workforce moving together. But give me the human version. What does it feel like to be the person orchestrating that speed? Is it flow state or controlled panic? What's the difference between Shawn-speed and reckless-speed?

24. **Digital twins and real crashes.** You wrote about simulation before theater. Why does simulation matter so much to a guy who survived a real crash that no simulation could have predicted? Is there a connection between wanting to model everything and having lived through the unmodelable?

25. **What "non-negotiable" actually costs you.** You have your minimums — 7 hours sleep, 4 dinners, bridge hour. What happens when the deal or the build pushes against those? Give me a specific time you had to choose the minimum over the momentum. What did it cost? What did it save?

## CHAPTER 5: "RELEASE NOTES" — Identity, Honesty, Legacy

These are the deepest ones. Save them for when you're most relaxed.

26. **The couch.** In "The Day I Couldn't Hide Anymore," you slept on the couch after your wife confronted you. Take me back to that couch. What were you staring at? What was running through your head? When did "I need help" become louder than "I can fix this myself"?

27. **Dr. Ellis's waiting room.** You're a CEO who solves problems for a living, sitting in a therapist's waiting room twisting a tissue. What was the voice in your head saying? What part of you wanted to leave? What part of you stayed?

28. **Counting Shawns — the integration.** You numbered your past selves: #5 grunge, #9 invisible, #11 polished. Sitting in Anguilla right now, post-massage, kids nearby, wife incrementally closer — what number is this Shawn? Or have you stopped counting? What does integration actually feel like versus what you thought it would feel like?

29. **Letter to Gia.** If you could only leave your daughter one page — not about business, not about EnergiAcres, just about how to live — what would it say? Talk it out. Don't think about it being good. Just say what you'd want her to know if you weren't here tomorrow.

30. **Letter to ten-year-old Shawn.** The kid in the hospital bed after the accident. 40 surgeries ahead of him. What do you say to that kid from this villa in Anguilla? What does he need to hear? What would surprise him most about where he ended up?

31. **What are you still afraid of?** Right now, in March 2026, with all of this — the family, the build, the book, the sun — what still scares you? What's the thing you haven't said out loud yet?

32. **"Foundry" — what it actually means.** You named this arc "Foundry." A foundry is where raw material gets melted down and recast into something new. What's being melted? What's being recast? What shape are you taking?

## BONUS: FAITH & THE UNNAMED

These are optional but they fill the weakest theme in the arc.

33. **The faith gap.** You mention losing the religious faith of your youth. What replaced it? Is EnergiAcres a kind of faith? Is the book? Is the family dinner table? What do you believe in now that you can't prove?

34. **40 surgeries and God.** When you were under anesthesia for the 20th time, the 30th time — was there a conversation happening with something bigger? Did it change over time? What does a man who's been cut open that many times think about when he's going under?

35. **The Sunday off-grid day.** You listed it as a non-negotiable. What does that day look like? Is it a sabbath? Is it just silence? What fills the space when you stop performing?

## HOW TO RECORD

- **Tool:** Voice Memos on your phone, or just hit record on any app
- **Length:** 5–10 minutes per question, more if it's flowing
- **Rules:** No editing. No restarting. If you stumble, keep going. The stumbles are often the best material.
- **When:** Morning, after coffee, before the beach. When your guard is down and your brain is fresh.
- **Send me:** The raw audio or a rough transcript. I'll turn it into drafts that sound like you on your best day.
