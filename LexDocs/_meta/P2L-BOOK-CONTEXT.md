**Theme:** Accident, surgeries, identity crisis
**Key facts:**
- **October 29, 1990.** Slightly cool fall day, leaves turning. Soccer championship won, pizza party next day. CJ "went into sales mode" — convinced 10-year-old Shawn to ride ATV alone to get gas. Pin fell out of wagon, no gas at farm, rushing home for McDonald's. No wagon = drove fast. "Time stood still. Eyes connected. Then nothing."
- Life-flighted to Akron Children's Hospital. Parents an hour away.

- Dad in ER: "Hands in his pockets. Looking down. Didn't say anything initially. Took it all in." Dad used to take out stitches at home.
- Mom stayed at Ronald McDonald House. Mom was there more. Dad took him to appointments. Shared effort.
- The gauze: "Most painful thing I've ever dealt with. Only time I've passed out from pain." Pulling dead flesh and gangrene.
- Playroom: Nintendo, toys ages 3-12. Bedridden kids not allowed. First time there — empty.
- The Drano boy: shared room with small boy who drank Drano, esophagus destroyed.
- 40+ surgeries. Latissimus dorsi from back to rebuild foot. Skin grafts from right leg.
- First steps: parents' bedroom, "brain told my legs to move, and it didn't happen, and I fell over."
- Told pastor at 10 God doesn't exist. Pastor never came back.
- ATV crash at 17: shattered left arm, 5 pins and a plate. Had to come clean with parents.
- Self-harm in high school: "Cut myself a lot so I could control the pain."
- Piercings: nipple rings, tongue — hidden for over a year. Dad found out at Grandpa Cutter's funeral (2001).
- Court 6 months after accident. Shawn testified. Insurance money for bills only — parents didn't ask for more.
- Last foot surgery 1999 → transferred to Miami University. Another surgery 2009 (Achilles + bone spur).
- University of Akron first (full scholarship, honors, chemistry = wrong). Transferred 1999. Graduated spring 2003 (150 credit hours, 5 years). MIS + Philosophy.

## WRITING STYLE

Each polished piece follows this pattern:
- **Evocative subtitle** (one line that hooks)
- **Sensory opening** (put the reader IN the moment — weather, smell, texture)
- **Reflective narrative** (Shawn's voice — direct, Feynman-style first principles, no bullshit)
- **"Cutter Rule" closing maxim** (one sentence wisdom distilled from the story)
- **Footer** with word count, arc placement, and notes on where it fits in the book
- **Target length:** 600-750 words for Medium pieces, 3000-5000 for book chapters
- **Voice:** Direct, reflective, systems-thinking, vulnerability through precision not sentimentality
