## THE DIAGNOSIS

### What You Have

The Forging arc covers 18 years of career — from college graduation through founding Fielding Systems, the Quorum acquisition, and the launch of Phase 2 Labs. It's your second-largest arc at 25 stories and ~29K words.

| Layer | Stories | Words | Feel |
|-------|---------|-------|------|
| **The Timeline Monster** | 1 story | 11,505 | "Shawn Cutter Timeline" — a massive chronological dump of everything |
| **Chapter drafts** | 8 stories | ~6,000 | Chapters 5-9 — skeletal outlines, 200-700 words each |
| **Strong vignettes** | 4 stories | ~3,200 | Night We Moved the Oilfield, Mud and Motherboards, Blackout on Two Grids, We Priced It Like a Well |
| **Memoir outline** | 1 story | 4,325 | Full book outline document |
| **Misplaced from other arcs** | 6 stories | ~3,300 | Foundry technical pieces tagged "forging" |
| **Meta / analysis** | 5 stories | ~900 | Psychoanalysis, outlines, fragments |

**The main problem:** This arc is the most *outlined* and least *written*. You have extensive chronological notes (the 11K-word Timeline is basically a biography) and skeleton chapters, but almost no immersive memoir scenes. The one exception — "The Night We Moved the Oilfield to the Cloud" — is excellent and proves you can write this material in your real voice. But it's surrounded by outlines that read like LinkedIn bios.

**The second problem:** The chapter drafts (5 through 9) are only 200-700 words each and read like section headers with bullet points. They cover the most dramatic business stories of your career — founding Fielding, the acquisition, the Quorum politics, the exit — but they're all "tell" and no "show."

### What's Strong

- **The Night We Moved the Oilfield to the Cloud** — Your best piece in this arc. The garage office, the 2:17 AM moment, the pumper's "yep." This is the template for how every Forging story should be written.
- **Mud and Motherboards** — Strong farm-to-tech bridge. Sensory and grounded.
- **Blackout on Two Grids** — Good parallel between power failure and personal burnout.
- **We Priced It Like a Well, Not a Website** — Farm logic applied to software pricing. Tight and original.
- **The Forty Percent Ask** — Bridge from Fractured to Forging. Body as leverage.
- **The Timeline document** — While it's not memoir, it's an incredibly valuable reference. Every major event from 1987 to present is logged.

### What's Missing

1. **The founding scene.** Fielding Systems started in your garage. Where's the scene? Who was there? What was on the whiteboard? What did the first customer call feel like?

2. **The marriage.** "Miami Merger" — you married your college sweetheart in 2006. The book barely mentions her courtship or the wedding. This is a major relationship arc.

3. **Gia's birth.** 2010. Your first child. Referenced in the timeline but has no story. How did becoming a father change the man who was always building?

4. **The Quorum acquisition — the human cost.** Chapter 8 is 679 words of skeleton. Where's the story of what it felt like to sell the thing you built? The negotiation. The moment you signed. The day after.

5. **The corporate years.** Chapters 6-7 cover Quorum and "the corporate labyrinth" but they're outlines. This is where "Innovation vs. Inertia" lives — the friction between a builder and a bureaucracy. You have the themes but no scenes.

6. **Dad's death.** 2010 — Chuck dies. It's in the timeline. It's the single most important event in the Forging arc (arguably the entire book), and it has NO dedicated story in this arc. The eulogy material lives in "Short Stories" under the Forged arc. It needs to be here, expanded, and it needs to be the emotional hinge of the entire arc.

7. **The pilot's license.** 2021 — Dad was your first passenger. Except Dad was dead by then. What does that mean? Is it a metaphor? A typo in the timeline? Either way, the pilot's license story is missing.

## MORNING RECORDING PROMPTS — FORGING ARC

### COLLEGE & MEETING YOUR WIFE

1. **Miami University — the first semester.** You walked onto campus as a kid with 40 surgeries, no faith, and a farm vocabulary. What was that transition like? Were you the only country kid? What surprised you about college life?

2. **Philosophy classes.** You studied MIS and Philosophy — an unusual combination. Tell me about the first philosophy class that changed how you thought. What was the professor like? What was the text? When did you realize philosophy wasn't just academic — it was survival equipment?

3. **Meeting your wife.** How did you meet? What was she like? What did she see in a guy with that much scar tissue (literal and otherwise)? Tell me the early days — the courtship, the moment you knew.

4. **The wedding.** 2006 — the Miami Merger. What do you remember? What was Chuck like at your wedding? What did your mom say?

### BUILDING FIELDING

5. **The garage.** Fielding Systems started in your garage apartment. Describe it for me. What was on the walls? What equipment? What time did you start working? What time did you stop? What did it smell like?

6. **The first customer.** Who was the first person who paid you money for Fielding software? How did you get them? What was the pitch? What did "yes" feel like?

7. **"Yep."** You wrote about the field hand texting "yep" when the data matched. That was the moment. But take me into the *before* — the months of broken modems, stuttering radios, ghost vendors. What was the lowest point before the breakthrough?

8. **The team.** Who were the people in that garage with you? Not titles — names. Faces. Who stayed until 2 AM? Who believed when it looked crazy? What happened to them?

9. **The Cutter Oil advantage.** You built on the family business first. What was it like pitching your dad's company on your software? Did Chuck get it? Did he push back? What did he say when it worked?

### DAD'S DEATH

10. **The call.** 2010. How did you find out Chuck died? Where were you? Who called? What were the first words? What did your body do?

11. **The drive / the flight.** What was the trip to get back like? What were you thinking? What song was on the radio? What did the world look like through the window?

12. **The funeral.** What do you remember? Who spoke? What did you say that you wrote versus what came out in the moment? Was the eulogy in "Short Stories" what you actually delivered?

13. **The first morning after.** The day after you buried Chuck. What did you do? Did you go to the farm? Did you work? When did the weight hit?

14. **The unfinished conversation.** You wrote "We never got to say goodbye or share these feelings." What would you have said? What do you think he would have said back?

### GIA

15. **The day Gia was born.** 2010 — the same year Chuck died. A death and a birth in the same year. Tell me about holding your daughter for the first time. What did you feel? Did Chuck get to meet her?

16. **The first time you sounded like Chuck.** Every parent has that moment where their dad's voice comes out of their mouth. When did it happen? What did you say? Were you horrified or proud?

### THE ACQUISITION

17. **The Quorum offer.** When did you first hear that someone wanted to buy Fielding? What was the number? What was the first conversation with your wife about it?

18. **The negotiation.** Take me into the room. Who was across the table? What did they want? What did you fight for? What did you give up?

19. **Signing day.** The moment you signed the papers that sold the thing you built in your garage. What pen? What room? What did you feel — relief, grief, triumph, emptiness? All of it?

20. **The corporate labyrinth.** You stayed at Quorum as VP. What was the first day like going from founder to employee? When did the friction start? What was the first moment you thought "this isn't my company anymore"?

21. **When Equity Didn't Move the Needle.** You have a story with this title. Go deeper. What happened when you tried to use equity to motivate people who didn't think like founders? What did you learn about the limits of incentives?

### THE BRIDGE TO FOUNDRY

22. **Phase 2 Labs.** 2018 — you start a family office venture foundry. What's the vision? Why "Phase 2"? What does the name mean to you? Is this the bridge between selling companies and building something permanent?

23. **The pilot's license.** Your timeline says Dad was your first passenger — but Dad died in 2010 and you got your license in 2021. Was it a metaphorical flight? An empty seat? Tell me the real story of why you learned to fly, and what it felt like to go up alone.

24. **The sentence that launched EnergiAcres.** Somewhere between the Quorum years and EnergiAcres, the food-energy-compute convergence clicked. But it didn't happen at a desk. Where were you? Was it gradual or sudden? This is the bridge scene between Arc 3 and Arc 4.
