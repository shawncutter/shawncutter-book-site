# Character Photos — P2L Book Project

## How to Use

1. **Drop photos into the right folder.** Each character has their own folder below.
2. **Name photos descriptively** — e.g., `chuck-1985-truck.jpg`, `beth-garden-2024.jpg`, `shawn-gia-first-day.jpg`
3. **Multiple photos welcome** — different eras, different contexts. The more the better.
4. **Tell Claude to process them** — say "process the photos" or "extract descriptions from photos" and Claude will read each image, generate a physical description, and update `characters.json`.

## What Claude Extracts From Each Photo

- **Physical description** — height estimate, build, hair, face, distinguishing features
- **Era/age estimate** — when the photo was likely taken
- **Setting/context** — where they are, what they're doing
- **Expression/energy** — what the photo says about who they are
- **Clothing/style** — what they wear, how they carry themselves
- **Writing details** — sensory details a writer would use (hands, posture, eyes, jawline, etc.)

## Photo Tips

- **Candid > posed** — candid shots reveal character better than portraits
- **Multiple eras** — a photo of Chuck in 1985 AND 2008 shows how he changed
- **Context matters** — Chuck next to a truck, Beth in the garden, Shawn at a desk
- **Group photos work too** — label who's who when you upload
- **Phone photos are fine** — resolution doesn't need to be perfect
